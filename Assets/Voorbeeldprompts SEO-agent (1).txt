Template 2: best practices
Parts and elements to optimise for better rankings:


0. Check for the obvious:
* are there internal or external links on the page?
* is the meta title 50 to 65 characters long?
* is the meta description between 140 and 155 characters long?
* do the images have proper titles and alt-text?
* is the main focus keyword present in the:
   * meta title
   * meta description
   * title (H1)
   * intro
   * 2 subheaders
   * last paragraph
* Does the text has a minimum of 1.000 words?


I. Core Foundational Elements
* Keyword & Intent Review:

   * [ ] Is there a clearly defined primary target keyword for this article? (Ensures focus for optimization)
   * [ ] Have relevant secondary keywords and related terms been considered for the content? (Helps cover the topic comprehensively)
   * [ ] Does the article's content type, format, and angle clearly match the likely search intent for the primary keyword? (e.g., an informational guide for a "how-to" query). Check what types of content rank on Google for your keyword. (Crucial for user satisfaction and ranking potential)
   * URL Optimization:

      * [ ] Is the URL concise and descriptive of the article's topic? (Aids user and search engine understanding)
      * [ ] Does the URL include the primary keyword naturally? (Strong relevance signal)
      * [ ] Does the URL use hyphens (-) to separate words (avoid underscores or spaces)? (Standard for readability and crawlability)
      * [ ] Does the URL use only lowercase letters? (Consistency and avoids potential technical issues)
      * [ ] Is the URL using HTTPS? (Fundamental security and trust signal)
II. Content Optimization
      * Quality, Value, Uniqueness:

         * [ ] Is the content unique and original? (Avoids penalties, provides value)
         * [ ] Is the content comprehensive and in-depth for the topic, fully addressing user needs? (Helps establish topical authority)
         * [ ] Does the content offer unique value or perspective (e.g., original examples, insights, data)? (Makes the content stand out)
         * [ ] Is the content factually accurate and well-researched? (Enhances trustworthiness)
         * [ ] Is the content up-to-date? (Check for and update any outdated information). (Maintains relevance and freshness)
         * Search Intent Alignment:

            * [ ] Does the content directly answer the core question or address the need implied by the primary keyword? (Satisfies user intent, reduces bounce rate)
            * E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness):

               * [ ] If applicable (e.g., reviews, guides, YMYL topics), does the content show Experience (e.g., personal stories, original photos)? (Demonstrates first-hand knowledge)
               * [ ] Does the content demonstrate Expertise through depth of knowledge and clear explanations? (Builds credibility)
               * [ ] If applicable, is author information (bio, credentials relevant to the topic) clearly displayed and linked? (Provides transparency and signals expertise)
               * [ ] Is the content trustworthy (accurate, clear sourcing if needed)? (Fundamental for credibility)
               * Natural Keyword Integration:

                  * [ ] Is the primary keyword included naturally within the first paragraph (or first ~150 words)? (Helps search engines and users quickly grasp the topic)
                  * [ ] Are the primary and secondary keywords integrated naturally throughout the body text where relevant? (Provides context and relevance signals)
                  * [ ] Are synonyms, variations, and related terms used (not just repeating the exact keyword)? (Reflects natural language and semantic understanding)
                  * [ ] Is keyword stuffing avoided? Does the text read naturally and provide a good user experience? (Avoids penalties and frustration)
                  * Readability & Formatting:

                     * [ ] Is the content broken into short, easily digestible paragraphs? (Improves scannability and readability)
                     * [ ] Are headings (H2, H3, etc.) used effectively to structure the content into logical sections? (Aids navigation for users and understanding for search engines)
                     * [ ] Are bullet points or numbered lists used where appropriate (e.g., for lists, steps, key takeaways)? (Enhances scannability and clarity)
                     * [ ] Are relevant images, videos, or other visuals included? (Breaks up text, improves engagement, illustrates points)
                     * [ ] Is formatting (bolding, italics) used effectively and sparingly to highlight key points? (Aids scannability and emphasis)
                     * [ ] Is there sufficient white space around text and elements for a clean layout? (Improves readability and user comfort)
                     * [ ] For long articles, is there a Table of Contents? (Aids user navigation)
III. Crucial HTML Tag Optimization
                     * Title Tag (<title>):

                        * [ ] Is the title tag unique for this page? (Important for differentiation in search results)
                        * [ ] Is the title tag length approximately 50-60 characters (check a SERP preview if possible)? (Avoids truncation in most search results)
                        * [ ] Does the title tag include the primary keyword, preferably near the beginning? (Strongest relevance signal in SERPs)
                        * [ ] Is the title tag compelling and accurate to encourage clicks from search results? (Influences Click-Through Rate - CTR)
                        * [ ] Is your brand name included appropriately (e.g., at the end, separated by a hyphen or pipe)? (Branding and trust signal)
                        * Meta Description (<meta name="description">):

                           * [ ] Is the meta description unique for this page? (Helps differentiate in search results)
                           * [ ] Is the meta description length approximately 150-160 characters (consider ~120 for mobile)? (Avoids truncation in most search results)
                           * [ ] Does the meta description include the primary keyword naturally? (Can be bolded by Google if it matches the user query, increasing relevance)
                           * [ ] Does it provide a clear, compelling summary of the page content? (Encourages users to click)
                           * [ ] Does it include a relevant Call to Action (CTA) if appropriate? (Prompts user action)
                           * [ ] Does it accurately reflect the actual content of the page? (Avoids user frustration and high bounce rates)
                           * Header Tags (<h1>-<h6>):

                              * [ ] Is there exactly ONE <h1> tag on the page? (Signals the main topic of the entire page)
                              * [ ] Does the <h1> tag contain the primary keyword and accurately describe the overall topic? (Strongest topic signal)
                              * [ ] Are <h2>, <h3>, etc., used in a logical, sequential hierarchy to structure content? (Avoid skipping levels, e.g., H1 straight to H3). (Creates a clear outline for users and search engines)
                              * [ ] Do headings accurately describe the content in their respective sections? (Improves scannability and understanding)
                              * [ ] Are relevant keywords and variations included naturally within headings? (Signals the topic of each section)
                              * Image Alt Text (alt=):

                                 * [ ] Do all meaningful images have descriptive alt text? (Crucial for accessibility and helps search engines understand image content)
                                 * [ ] Is the alt text concise (ideally under 125 characters)? (Better for screen readers)
                                 * [ ] Does the alt text include relevant keywords naturally if they accurately describe the image? (Relevant for image search and page context)
                                 * [ ] Does the alt text avoid phrases like "image of..."? (Redundant for screen readers)
                                 * [ ] Are purely decorative images (e.g., line breaks, simple icons) using an empty alt="" attribute? (Improves experience for screen reader users by skipping non-essential images)
                                 * Image Filename:

                                    * [ ] Is the image filename descriptive of the image content? (Provides a relevance signal)
                                    * [ ] Does the filename use hyphens (-) to separate words? (Standard practice)
                                    * [ ] Does the filename include relevant keywords where appropriate? (Additional relevance signal)
IV. Strategic Linking
                                    * Internal Linking:

                                       * [ ] Does this article link out to other relevant pages on your website? (Helps users discover more content, distributes authority within your site)
                                       * [ ] Are there relevant internal links pointing to this article from other pages on your site? (May require checking other pages). (Important for discoverability by search engines and passing authority)
                                       * [ ] Is descriptive, relevant anchor text used for internal links? (Provides context for the linked page)
                                       * [ ] Are internal links placed naturally within the content flow? (Good user experience)
                                       * External Linking (Outbound Links):

                                          * [ ] Does the article link out to credible, authoritative, and relevant external sources where appropriate (e.g., to cite data, provide further reading)? (Builds credibility and trust, supports E-E-A-T)
                                          * [ ] Is descriptive anchor text used for external links? (Provides context for the external page)
                                          * [ ] Do external links open in a new browser tab/window (target="_blank")? (Keeps users on your site while they explore the external resource)
                                          * [ ] Are rel="nofollow", rel="sponsored", or rel="ugc" attributes used correctly for applicable external links (e.g., sponsored content, user comments)? (Communicates the nature of the link to search engines)
                                          * [ ] Have external links been checked to ensure they are not broken (404s)? (May require a simple link checker tool). (Avoids poor user experience and potential negative signals)

Rules for Translation:

-Use regional adjustments for geographical references: Replace terms referring to countries or languages in search queries (e.g., "Netherlands" or "Dutch") with the correct name for each country (e.g., "England" in English, "España" in Spanish).

-Include both long, grammatically correct variations and short, informal search terms. Use synonyms and alternative formulations that match natural search behavior in each language.
Example: Dutch users may search for "SEO specialist Nederland," while the grammatically correct form is "SEO-specialist in the Netherlands." Include both variants in your translation for all languages. This may result in significantly longer keyword lists for some languages than for others.
-Add both singular and plural forms to your translations.
-Consider the target audience (e.g., professionals or general users).
-Include variations that match specific search intents (informational, navigational, transactional).
-Adapt transactional keywords to reflect regional purchase behavior and cultural preferences.
-Adapt terms that are specific to the topic or theme (e.g., technical terms or industry jargon).
-Go beyond literal translations and, based on the context of the keywords, consider whether alternative translations might apply.
-Account for compound words in languages like German and for more formal structures in French or Italian. Include different variations.

-Check for potential cultural differences in search behavior.
-Consider semantic search. For example, if translating the word "spijkerbroek" (Dutch for "jeans"), include related terms like "jeans" in all possible variants of your translation.
-If the topic requires specific context (e.g., "cow health" or "technology"), use that context to tailor the keywords to the theme.


Presentation of Keywords:

-Begin with the language name, followed by the list of keywords underneath.
-Use a clear structure to ensure easy copying and pasting.
